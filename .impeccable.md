## Brand Family

This dApp ships under the **Lazy Superheroes (LSH)** umbrella and is hosted
on the lazysuperheroes domain. LSH is a character-driven NFT/IP/games
project on Hedera. The umbrella branding spec is vendored locally at
[`docs/LSH-Branding-Reference.md`](docs/LSH-Branding-Reference.md) (canonical
source: https://docs.lazysuperheroes.com/docs/branding_guide). For "what
does *family* feel like?" calibration, the sister product `lazylotto-agent`
carries the *playful, bold, community* register — its design context is
snapshot at [`docs/LSH-SISTER-PROJECT-CONTEXT.md`](docs/LSH-SISTER-PROJECT-CONTEXT.md).

**This product is intentionally different.** hedera-multisig is a financial
signing tool for serious operators — not a game, not a community portal, not
a token product. The LSH branding guide's "earned excitement" principle
explicitly says *"don't make withdrawing funds feel like a slot machine"* —
that calm-financial register IS the entire job here.

**What we share with the LSH umbrella:**
- **Primary action color**: blue `#3b82f6` (matches LSH `primary`).
- **Status semantics**: success `#16a34a`, warning `#f59e0b`, info `#0ea5e9`,
  destructive `#dc2626`. Sacred meanings — never decorative.
- **Brand attribution**: every page footer carries a "By Lazy Superheroes"
  line with the LSH wordmark/logo (logo URLs and favicon listed in
  [`docs/LSH-Branding-Reference.md`](docs/LSH-Branding-Reference.md) §2)
  linking to lazysuperheroes.com.
- **LAZY Gold `#e5a800`** is *reserved* — only appears in LSH-attribution
  contexts (footer wordmark, "made by" link). It must NEVER appear in
  functional UI of this dApp. We do not have a token; the gold is not ours.
- **No emoji-as-icons in functional UI** (LSH-wide rule). Use a clean icon
  set; Material Symbols Outlined is the LSH default and a reasonable choice
  here too.

**Where we diverge intentionally:**
- LSH lazylotto is *dark-mode-only, playful, gaming-energetic*. We are
  *light-default, calm-financial, dual-register*. The dev mode below
  (terminal/punk) is the deliberate technical-operator divergence; it is
  NOT the LSH consumer aesthetic and shouldn't try to be.
- LSH typography: Heebo + Unbounded. Treasury mode SHOULD adopt these for
  family unity (Heebo body, Unbounded for the few display moments). Dev
  mode is free to choose a more terminal-aligned grotesque + monospace
  stack (e.g., Geist + Geist Mono are already loaded; JetBrains Mono /
  Berkeley Mono are also acceptable choices). Whatever the dev-mode
  display face is, it should NOT be Inter and SHOULD feel less polished
  and more functional than the treasury face.
- LSH decorative typefaces (Press Start 2P, Viga `.lazy-font`, Orbitron,
  etc.) are out of scope here — this is not a game and they would feel
  costume-y in a financial signing context.

## Design Context

### Users

Two audiences, served by **two switchable visual themes** of the same product —
not two separate apps, not just light/dark. The theme switch communicates which
audience the operator self-identifies with and changes the entire aesthetic
register accordingly.

- **Default — Treasury operators (mainnet stakes).** Finance and ops teams
  moving real money on Hedera. They arrive in a serious mood: signing is the
  job, and they want the interface to *get out of the way* and *feel
  trustworthy enough to bet on*. Often arriving via a connection-string link
  in Slack or email; the dApp may be the first time they've seen anything
  Hedera-shaped. Mainnet treasury teams default into this mode.
- **Crypto-native — Devs and agent builders.** Comfortable with terminals,
  CLIs, and dense data. They've shipped on EVM chains, are evaluating Hedera
  for agent or scheduled-tx workflows, and *want* the dApp to telegraph that
  it's a serious tool, not a banking portal. Toggle reveals a punk / terminal
  register: tighter density, sharper motion, accent neon, dark-first.

The job to be done is the same in both modes: **coordinate a multi-party
signing ceremony, show what's about to be signed, prove the network confirmed
it.** The themes change tone, not function.

### Brand Personality

- **Treasury mode (default):** *calm, precise, trustworthy.* Premium-financial.
  Closest references: **Stripe, Mercury, Linear marketing.** Generous
  whitespace, subdued palette, careful typography, restrained motion. The
  design should feel like a place where money moves carefully.
- **Dev mode (toggle):** *sharp, technical, confident.* Engineer-tool. Closest
  references: **Vercel, Railway, Resend.** Punk-adjacent — neon accents on
  near-black, denser layouts, terminal-cadence interactions, comfortable with
  showing more raw data. Aggressive in restraint, not in decoration.

Both modes share: **no AI slop fingerprints, no DeFi clichés, no marketing
gradients, no hero-metric layouts.**

### Aesthetic Direction

**Anchor — what to chase:**
- Treasury mode: refined-minimal. Tinted neutrals (cool slate or warm sand,
  not pure `#fff`/`#0a0a0a`), one ink accent (current blue-600 is fine),
  generous spacing, asymmetric layouts, distinctive but quiet type. Think
  *bank statement, well-printed.* Low motion budget.
- Dev mode: brutalist-terminal. Tighter modular type scale, monospace allowed
  but *not as decoration* — only where it carries meaning (hashes, keys,
  command snippets). Sharp accent (acid green / cyan / amber — pick one
  per session, not all three). Dark-first with carefully tinted neutrals
  (never `#000`). Motion is faster and more confident, never cute.

**Anti-references — what to actively avoid:**
- The AI-generated 2024-2025 look in all its forms: cyan-on-dark + purple
  gradients + glassmorphism + identical card grids + Inter everywhere +
  glowing borders + hero-metric numbers + rounded icons over every heading.
  This is the user's strongest no.
- Generic DeFi dashboards (gradient text, neon-on-dark used decoratively,
  three-column feature templates).
- Generic SaaS marketing pages (smiley illustrations, gradient hero text,
  rounded-icon-over-heading, identical 3-up feature cards).
- Bouncy or elastic motion. Decorative monospace. Pure black or pure white
  backgrounds. Default Inter. Default rounded-2xl drop-shadow cards.

**Theme toggle as feature, not afterthought:** the theme switch is part of
the brand. Make sure it sits somewhere obvious, communicates intent (not just
"light/dark"), and the transition between modes is one of the polished
moments of the app.

### Design Principles

1. **Trust beats delight.** Every choice should make a treasury operator feel
   *more* confident this is real engineering, not less. If a flourish doesn't
   help signers verify what they're signing, cut it.
2. **Two registers, one product.** Treasury and dev modes are equally first-class.
   Don't design treasury-first and skin a dark theme over the top — the dev
   mode should feel like its own intentional aesthetic from typography down,
   not just inverted colors.
3. **Asymmetry over symmetry.** Left-aligned headers, varied spacing, broken
   grids for emphasis. Centered-everything is the AI-slop default and we are
   explicitly past that.
4. **No card reflex.** Not every group needs a container with a border and a
   shadow. The current code already overuses `rounded-lg border bg-gray-50`
   patterns — flatten where the hierarchy is clear without it.
5. **Density is honesty.** Especially in dev mode, but also in treasury:
   transaction data, signature progress, mirror confirmation should be
   *legible*, not paraphrased into reassurance copy. Show the bytes, the
   hashes, the IDs — but with type discipline, not as a wall.
6. **Motion is signal, not decoration.** Use `ease-out-quart` (already in
   place); reserve animation for state transitions that signers need to
   perceive — countdown ticking, threshold-met handoff, mirror confirmation
   landing. Never decorative pulses, never bounces, never glow loops.
7. **Tinted neutrals, never pure.** `#fff` and `#000` never appear in nature
   and never in this dApp. Every neutral picks up a subtle hint of the active
   theme's accent — even 1–2% of hue mix is enough.
8. **Microcopy is part of the design.** Every label, every error, every empty
   state. "Session expired" is a placeholder; the production copy explains
   *what to do next.* The recent F-phase tinybar humanization
   (`100000000 (1.00000000 ℏ)`) is the standard.

### Notes for future sessions

- Geist Sans + Geist Mono are loaded; treat them as a starting kit, not a
  commitment. A more distinctive display face is welcome in either mode if
  it earns its place.
- Tailwind v4 with `@theme inline` is the styling layer. CSS custom
  properties already declared in `app/globals.css` for `--background` /
  `--foreground` / `--font-sans` / `--font-mono` and animation easings.
  Class-based dark mode via `.dark` is wired up; the dev-mode/treasury-mode
  toggle should layer cleanly on top of that.
- `prefers-reduced-motion` is honored globally in `globals.css` — keep it.
- WAI-ARIA is taken seriously: tablists in `/create`, `tabular-nums` on
  countdowns, focus-visible rings everywhere. Don't regress this for visual
  cleverness.
